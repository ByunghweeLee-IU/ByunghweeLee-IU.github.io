About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the School of Data Science, University of Virginia, where I work with Yong-Yeol Ahn. My research focuses on computational methods for studying human beliefs, social dynamics, art and culture, and large-scale digital traces.

Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Researcher at Indiana University, working with Jisun An, Haewoon Kwak, and Yong-Yeol Ahn, and I also worked as a postdoctoral-level senior researcher at Sungkyunkwan University with Beom Jun Kim. I received my PhD in Physics from KAIST under Hawoong Jeong, where my dissertation examined art and complexity in the era of big data, and I also earned my BS in Physics from the KAIST Department of Physics.
