Byunghwee Lee (이병휘) is a data scientist and computational social scientist who develops interpretable, quantitative frameworks for understanding human behavior, beliefs, and creativity.

He is currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Virginia School of Data Science, working with Yong-Yeol Ahn. His research integrates machine learning, large language models, and network science to study human beliefs, cultural expression, and collective social dynamics from large-scale cultural and behavioral data.

Core Questions#

  1. How can beliefs be represented and modeled?
    How can human beliefs and cognition be meaningfully represented in high-dimensional spaces, and how do beliefs evolve, interact, and change within those spaces?
  2. How can creativity and cultural expression be quantified?
    How can creativity and cultural expression be modeled from data at scale, and how do artistic forms emerge, evolve, and become expressed across historical, social, and technological contexts?

Current Research Focus#

  • Interpretable belief representation and belief dynamics with large language models
  • Computational analysis of art history, creativity, and multimodal cultural data
  • Network science approaches to collective behaviors in online media and social systems
