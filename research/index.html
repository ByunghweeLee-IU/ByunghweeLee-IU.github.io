Research

My research lies at the intersection of AI, data science, and society. I develop computational methods to study culture, belief, and collective behavior using large-scale observational data.

Research Themes#

AI, Human Beliefs, and Political Judgment#

I study how large language models can represent human belief systems, political judgment, and semantic variation in public discourse. This line of work connects representation learning, computational social science, and human-centered AI.

Art, Culture, and Creativity#

I use information theory, multimodal learning, and cultural data to study long-term patterns in art history, stylistic change, and visual creativity. This includes work on landscape painting, user-generated art, and multimodal AI for cultural analysis.

Networks, Media, and Social Dynamics#

I examine online polarization, media ecosystems, and networked social behavior. My work in this area includes research on news press structure, discourse around epidemics, and hidden dependencies in weighted networks.

Selected Projects#

  • Belief embedding spaces based on large language models
  • Political inference from non-political online conversations
  • Network analysis of news media and user polarization
  • Information-theoretic analysis of painting and cultural evolution
  • Multimodal AI for tracing hidden pathways in art history
