The Annals of the Joseon Dynasty#

Mapping five centuries of Korean history

This visualization maps the geographic locations mentioned in the Annals of the Joseon Dynasty, tracing how places emerge across five centuries of Korean history through historical records.

Data curation and visualization by Byunghwee Lee and Daniel Kim. (2015).

Project Notes#

  • This project explores how historical texts can be transformed into spatial narratives.
  • The visualization highlights the geographic distribution of places mentioned in the Joseon Dynasty annals over time.
  • The place entities were collected using location tags embedded in the HTML of the Annals of the Joseon Dynasty. Their geographic coordinates (latitude and longitude) were manually annotated.
  • It reveals which locations are most prominently mentioned in the historical record.

Temporal Changes in Frequently Mentioned Cities in the Annals of the Joseon Dynasty#

  • A clear centralization toward Seoul is observed, with increasing mentions of cities around Seoul and a corresponding decline in mentions of peripheral regions.

Locations Mentioned with Three Historical Figures (Yi Sun-sin, Kim Jong-seo, Jeong Yak-yong)#

  • This section shows the geographic locations mentioned in the same records as each historical figure in the Annals.

  • Regions with higher density of associated locations indicate the spatial context and influence of each individual.
